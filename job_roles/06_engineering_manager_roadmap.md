# Engineering Manager & Team Lead Roadmap (6 Weeks)

Target Audience: Current or aspiring Engineering Managers, Staff Engineers taking on leadership duties, and Delivery Managers.
Primary Focus: People Management, Execution, System Architecture (for Leaders), Soft Skills, Cross-functional alignment.

## Overview
This 6-week roadmap pivots entirely from raw coding towards people leadership, delivery metrics, organizational design, and architectural consensus. It leverages `Social_Communication_Skills`, `Scenariobase_questions_bank`, `Business_To_Tech`, and `system_design`.

---

## Week 1: Team Building & People Management
*Goal: Fostering a high-performing engineering culture.*

* **Day 1-2: Hiring & Onboarding**  
  * **Resource**: `Social_Communication_Skills`.
  * **Action**: Designing technical rubrics. Assessing for cultural add vs cultural fit. 30-60-90 day onboarding plans for Junior vs Senior engineers.
* **Day 3-4: Feedback & 1-on-1s**  
  * **Action**: The radical candor framework. Structuring effective 1-on-1s. Giving constructive feedback on underperformance.
* **Day 5-6: Career Pathing & Mentorship**  
  * **Action**: Promoting engineers. Helping a Senior Engineer transition into an Architect, or managing an engineer who wants your job.
* **Day 7: Performance Reviews**  
  * **Action**: Calibrating performance properly. Managing PIPs (Performance Improvement Plans).

## Week 2: Agile Delivery & Execution
*Goal: Delivering software predictably.*

* **Day 1-2: Agile Methodologies at Scale**  
  * **Action**: Scrum vs Kanban. Velocity metrics, burn-down charts, story pointing.
* **Day 3-4: Tech Debt vs Feature Delivery**  
  * **Resource**: `Business_To_Tech`.
  * **Action**: Strategies for negotiating tech debt sprints with Product Managers. Utilizing the Rule of 20% refactoring.
* **Day 5-6: Managing Scope Creep & Deadlines**  
  * **Action**: Prioritization frameworks (MoSCoW, RICE). Communicating delays transparently to stakeholders.
* **Day 7: Incident Management**  
  * **Resource**: `Scenariobase_questions_bank`.
  * **Action**: Acting as Incident Commander during severity-1 outages. Conducting blameless post-mortems.

## Week 3: Systems Architecture for Leaders
*Goal: Retaining enough technical depth to steer technical decisions effectively.*

* **Day 1: High-Level Design (HLD) Review**  
  * **Resource**: `HLD`, `system_design`.
  * **Action**: Ensure you know how to validate a technical design document (RFC/ADR) provided by your lead engineers.
* **Day 2-3: Technical Strategy**  
  * **Action**: Monolith to Microservices decisions. When NOT to use microservices. Build vs Buy vs Open Source analyses.
* **Day 4-5: Evaluating Trade-offs**  
  * **Resource**: `architecture`.
  * **Action**: Balancing speed-to-market against long-term maintainability. Assessing the organizational cost of a polyglot architecture.
* **Day 6-7: Cloud Resourcing Strategy**  
  * **Resource**: `azure`.
  * **Action**: FinOps. Monitoring cloud costs and ensuring infrastructure efficiency.

## Week 4: Strategic Alignment (Business to Tech)
*Goal: Bridging the gap between the boardroom and the server room.*

* **Day 1-2: Translating Metrics**  
  * **Resource**: `Business_To_Tech`.
  * **Action**: Tying latency metrics to user retention, error rates to customer churn.
* **Day 3-4: Stakeholder Management**  
  * **Action**: Managing upwards to Directors/VPs. Managing sideways with peer Product Management and Design leads.
* **Day 5-6: Cross-Team Dependencies**  
  * **Action**: Resolving blocked dependencies across org limits without escalating unnecessarily.
* **Day 7: Budgeting & Headcount**  
  * **Action**: Creating a business justification for budget increases or additional headcount based on engineering necessity.

## Week 5: Scenario & Conflict Resolution Mocks
*Goal: Passing the behavioral and leadership interview loops.*

* **Day 1-2: Behavioral Framework (STAR/BSTAR)**  
  * **Resource**: `Scenariobase_questions_bank`.
  * **Action**: Structure answers defining Situation, Task, Action, Result, and Reflection.
* **Day 3-4: Conflict Resolution**  
  * **Resource**: `Social_Communication_Skills`.
  * **Action**: "Tell me about a time you managed a poorly performing engineer." "How did you resolve a fundamental architecture dispute between two tech leads?"
* **Day 5-6: Product Disputes**  
  * **Action**: "A PM demands a feature in 2 weeks that requires 4 weeks. What do you do?"
* **Day 7: Retrospective Practice**  
  * **Action**: "Tell me about a technical project that failed under your leadership. What did you learn?"

## Week 6: Presentation & Final Review
*Goal: Perfecting the System and People Design rounds.*

* **Day 1-3: System Design / Org Design Interviews**  
  * **Resource**: `system_design`.
  * **Action**: Practice designing not just the system, but the *teams* required to build the system (Conway's Law mapping).
* **Day 4-6: Mock Interviews with Peers**  
  * **Action**: Do 2 full mock loops. Focus on narrative, brevity, and business-value focus. Let your technical empathy show.
* **Day 7: Final Preparation**
