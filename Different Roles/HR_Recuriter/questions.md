Great move asking this—because **Technical Recruiter interviews are very predictable** if you prepare the right way.

They usually test you on 4 areas:
👉 Recruitment skills
👉 Technical understanding
👉 Communication & stakeholder handling
👉 Real scenarios

I’ll give you a **complete, structured list + how to think about answers** 👇

---

# 🎯 1. Most Common HR / Recruiter Questions

### 🔹 Basic Intro

* Tell me about yourself
* Why are you transitioning from Software Engineer to Technical Recruiter?
* Why recruitment and not continuing in development?

👉 Tip:
Focus on:

* Interview experience
* Interest in people + hiring
* Business impact

---

### 🔹 Motivation & Fit

* Why do you want to work in Talent Acquisition?
* What do you enjoy about interviewing candidates?
* Why should we hire you as a recruiter?

---

# 💼 2. Core Recruitment Questions (VERY IMPORTANT)

### 🔥 Sourcing & Screening

* How do you source candidates?
* What platforms do you use? (LinkedIn, Naukri, referrals)
* How do you shortlist candidates?

---

### 🔥 Interviewing

* How do you evaluate a candidate?
* What do you check in a technical interview?
* How do you assess communication skills?

---

### 🔥 Hiring Process

* Walk me through your hiring process
* How do you coordinate interviews?
* How do you handle multiple roles at once?

---

# 🧠 3. Technical Understanding Questions

Since you're from engineering, they WILL test this 👇

* Difference between frontend and backend developer?
* What skills do you look for in a backend developer?
* What is cloud? (basic understanding is enough)
* How do you evaluate a developer without coding yourself?

---

👉 They are checking:
✔ Can you talk to engineers confidently
✔ Can you judge candidates logically

---

# 🤝 4. Stakeholder / Business Questions

These are **very important for your profile**

* How do you work with hiring managers?
* What if hiring manager changes requirements?
* How do you handle disagreement with stakeholders?
* How do you understand job requirements?

---

👉 Best approach:
Show:

* Communication
* Clarity
* Business alignment

---

# ⚡ 5. Real Scenario-Based Questions (Most Important Round)

### 🔥 Very Common:

* What if a candidate rejects the offer?
* What if you can’t find candidates for a role?
* How do you handle multiple open positions?
* What if hiring is urgent?

---

### 💡 Tricky Ones:

* What if a candidate is technically strong but poor in communication?
* What if a hiring manager is too picky?
* How do you deal with candidate drop-offs?

---

👉 These questions decide selection.

---

# 📊 6. Metrics / Performance Questions

* How many interviews have you taken?
* How many candidates have you hired?
* What is your turnaround time?
* How do you measure hiring success?

---

👉 You should say:

* 100+ interviews
* Resume screening experience
* Hiring support

---

# 🧩 7. Behavioral Questions

* Tell me about a difficult candidate you handled
* Tell me about a conflict with a stakeholder
* Tell me about a time you failed

👉 Use **STAR method**:
Situation → Task → Action → Result

---

# 🚀 8. Questions YOU Should Ask Them

Always ask 2–3:

* What does success look like in this role?
* What kind of roles will I be hiring for?
* What challenges is your hiring team facing?
