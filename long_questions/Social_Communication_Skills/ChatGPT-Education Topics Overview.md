### 1. Interpersonal Communication

This is the most direct academic term. It is the study of how people exchange information, feelings, and meaning through verbal and non-verbal messages.

* **What it covers:** Why we choose certain words, how "mirroring" works, and how to reduce "uncertainty" when meeting new people.
* **Key Concept:** **Dyadic Communication** (communication specifically between two people).

### 2. Organizational Behavior (OB)

If you are interested in how to impress others in a **professional or office environment**, this is the topic for you.

* **What it covers:** Leadership styles, how to influence colleagues, and "Impression Management"—the conscious or subconscious process in which people attempt to influence the perceptions of others.

### 3. Soft Skills & Personality Development

This is the most common name for practical, non-academic courses found in training centers or online platforms (like Coursera, Udemy, or NPTEL).

* **What it covers:** Body language, active listening, public speaking, and "Quick-wittedness."
* **Key Model:** The **Johari Window** (a technique used to help people better understand their relationship with themselves and others).

### 4. Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

Often taught as a standalone subject, EQ is the "engine" behind an impressive personality.

* **What it covers:** Self-awareness (knowing how you come across) and Social Skills (managing the emotions of others during a conversation).
