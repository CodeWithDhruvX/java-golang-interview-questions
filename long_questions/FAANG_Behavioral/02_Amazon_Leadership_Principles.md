# Amazon Leadership Principles (LPs)

Amazon's interview process is famously rigid: **50% of your interview score is based entirely on the 16 Leadership Principles**. 
Every single interviewer is assigned 2-3 specific LPs to evaluate you on, and they will ask behavioral questions specifically designed to test those principles.

If you don't map your STAR stories to these principles, you will fail the Amazon loop, even if your code is perfect.

## The Most Critical "Core" LPs for Software Engineers
*(You must have at least one solid STAR story prepared for each of these)*

### 1. Customer Obsession
*Leaders start with the customer and work backwards.*
- **What they want to hear:** You went out of your way to understand the user's pain. You pushed back on a product requirement because it was bad for the user.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer.*
  - *Tell me about a time you disagreed with PM/Design because the customer experience was compromised.*

### 2. Deliver Results
*Leaders focus on the key inputs and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion.*
- **What they want to hear:** You hit a tight deadline. You overcame massive roadblocks. You didn't make excuses when things went wrong.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about a time you had to deliver a project on an impossible deadline.*
  - *Tell me about a time you had to overcome a major obstacle to get a project over the finish line.*

### 3. Ownership
*Leaders are owners. They never say "that's not my job."*
- **What they want to hear:** You found a bug in someone else's code and fixed it instead of ignoring it. You took responsibility for a team failure. You stepped up when a lead was out sick.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about a time you stepped outside your core responsibilities.*
  - *Tell me about a time you saw a problem and took the initiative to fix it without being asked.*

### 4. Dive Deep
*Leaders operate at all levels and stay connected to the details.*
- **What they want to hear:** You didn't just accept a surface-level answer. You dug into the raw server logs, you read the source code of a third-party library, you found the absolute root cause of a complex issue.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about the most complex technical problem you've ever solved.*
  - *Tell me about a time you had to dive deep into data or code to figure out what was really going on.*

### 5. Invent and Simplify
*Leaders expect and require innovation... and always find ways to simplify.*
- **What they want to hear:** You automated a tedious manual process. You refactored a massive monolithic mess into a clean, simple module. You replaced a complex 3rd party tool with a simple 50-line script.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about a time you simplified a complex process.*
  - *Tell me about a time you came up with a creative solution to a hard problem.*

### 6. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
*Leaders respectfully challenge decisions... but once a decision is determined, they commit wholly.*
- **What they want to hear:** You had a data-backed disagreement with your boss or team. You argued your case respectfully. (Crucially) you lost the argument, but you still worked 100% hard to implement their decision without acting bitter.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about a time you strongly disagreed with your manager.*
  - *Tell me about a time you pushed back on a decision, but ultimately had to commit to it.*

### 7. Earn Trust
*Leaders listen to others, speak candidly, and treat others with respect.*
- **What they want to hear:** You admitted a mistake. You took the blame for a production outage you caused. You repaired a broken relationship with an angry teammate.
- **Common Questions:**
  - *Tell me about a time you made a critical mistake. How did you handle it?*
  - *Tell me about a time you received harsh feedback from a peer.*

---

## Strategy for Amazon Loop
1. **Pre-write 5 "Master Stories"** using the STAR method.
2. **Map them to multiple LPs:** A story about hunting down a memory leak over the weekend covers *Dive Deep*, *Ownership*, and *Deliver Results*.
3. **Data is King:** Amazon wants metrics. "Reduced latency by 40%", "Saved $10k/month", "Decreased CI build time from 20 mins to 5 mins".
4. **Never blame others:** If your story involves a failure, take ownership of your part in it. "My teammate wrote bad code" is an instant rejection. Instead, say: "Our team's review process failed to catch an edge case, which I then..."
