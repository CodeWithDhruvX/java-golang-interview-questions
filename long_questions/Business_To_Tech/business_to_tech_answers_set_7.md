# Business to Tech Interview Questions & Answers - Set 7

## ðŸ”¹ 1. Technical Product Manager (Questions 601-612)

**Q601: How do you evaluate whether a business problem is worth building a new feature for?**
* **Headline:** "ROI calculation."
* **Example:** "Problem cost: $100/mo. Solution cost: $10k. ROI: Negative. Don't build."

**Q602: How do you balance short-term wins against long-term platform strategy?**
* **Headline:** "Tactical/Strategic Split."
* **Example:** "20% Tactical (Wins). 80% Strategic (Platform). We feed the business beast while building the future."

**Q603: How do you ensure cross-team alignment when multiple products interact?**
* **Headline:** "Integration Contracts."
* **Example:** "Team A and B sign the Swagger definition before writing code."

**Q604: How do you incorporate customer feedback into backlog prioritization?**
* **Headline:** "Weighted Feedback."
* **Example:** "Feedback from 'Enterprise' tag counts 5x more than 'Free User'."

**Q605: How do you decide which technical dependencies to address first?**
* **Headline:** "Blocker Analysis."
* **Example:** "If A blocks B, C, and D. Fix A first."

**Q606: How do you manage product decisions under uncertainty in market demand?**
* **Headline:** "Optionality."
* **Example:** "Build the 'Waitlist' page first. If 10k signups, build product."

**Q607: How do you ensure product experiments provide measurable insights?**
* **Headline:** "Control Group."
* **Example:** "A/B test. If you don't have a Control, you don't learned anything."

**Q608: How do you handle conflicting metrics between teams and leadership?**
* **Headline:** "Hierarchy of Truth."
* **Example:** "Leadership metric (Revenue) overrides Team metric (Velocity)."

**Q609: How do you decide when a product feature should be standardized versus customized?**
* **Headline:** "80/20 Rule."
* **Example:** "If 80% of users use it the same way, Standardize."

**Q610: How do you ensure handoffs between product and engineering are frictionless?**
* **Headline:** "Ready Definition."
* **Example:** "Ticket is not 'Ready' without: Design, Acceptance Criteria, and tracking tags."

**Q611: How do you handle stakeholder pressure to deliver technically infeasible features?**
* **Headline:** "Physics Lesson."
* **Example:** "I explain *Why* it is impossible simply. Then offer the 'Next Best Alternative'."

**Q612: How do you measure the business impact of incremental product improvements?**
* **Headline:** "Before/After."
* **Example:** "Measure baseline. Ship improvement. Measure delta. Attribute delta to ship."

**Q618: How do you evaluate when to refactor versus extend an existing system?**
* **Headline:** "Tipping Point."
* **Example:** "If extending takes 2x longer than writing new, Refactor."

**Q619: How do you manage cross-team architectural dependencies?**
* **Headline:** "Service Registry."
* **Example:** "Central list of who owns what API. No orphan APIs."

**Q620: How do you handle design conflicts between business and technical objectives?**
* **Headline:** "Trade-off Document."

**Q621: How do you ensure solutions are compliant with industry regulations?**
* **Headline:** "Legal Review."

**Q622: How do you evaluate multiple architectural options under time constraints?**
* **Headline:** "Timeboxed POC."

**Q623: How do you communicate trade-offs in technical design to non-technical stakeholders?**
* **Headline:** "House Analogy."
* **Example:** "We can build a Mansion (Slow) or a Cabin (Fast). You chose Fast, so you get a Cabin."

**Q624: How do you ensure future growth is supported by todayâ€™s architectural choices?**
