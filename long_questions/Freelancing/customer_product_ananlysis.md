
This video addresses the common concern that web development might be obsolete by 2026 due to advancements in AI. The speaker argues that while AI tools like ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, and Antigravity can automate basic website creation (0:21-1:14), they lack the critical thinking and strategic understanding required for effective business-oriented websites (2:20-2:29).

Here's a breakdown of the key points:

Automation of Web Development (0:00-1:22): AI tools can generate entire websites, including front-end (React Native) and back-end (Python FastAPI, PostgreSQL) components, in a fraction of the time it previously took (0:50-1:14). This has led to concerns about job displacement for web developers.
The True Purpose of a Website (2:00-2:15): For a business, a website is not just an information display but a "conversion point" designed to attract customers and generate leads.
The Role of a Skilled Web Developer (2:29-3:51): A true web developer understands business goals, audience behavior, and user profiles to create a strategic website plan. They focus on "conversion rate optimization" (CRO), guiding users through a flow to become customers or fill out lead forms (3:15-3:27).
Repositioning Yourself (3:52-5:12): Developers should market themselves as creators of "conversion-oriented websites" rather than just "websites" (3:57-4:03). They should also learn to integrate AI into their workflow to build faster, smarter, and more creative high-end websites using tools like Three.js, WebGL, and GSAP (4:18-4:51).

The video concludes that web development is not dead but evolving, emphasizing that developers who leverage AI and focus on strategic, conversion-focused design will remain in high demand (4:54-5:12).
